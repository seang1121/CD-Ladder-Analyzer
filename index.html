ü™ú What is a CD Ladder?

A CD ladder divides your money into multiple CDs with different maturity dates. You get automatic access to portions of your money on a schedule instead of having everything locked up for years.

‚ùå Problem with Single CD:

  • ‚úó $50K in 1 CD @ 5.45% for 5 years = completely locked
  • ‚úó Can't access funds if you need them
  • ‚úó Miss better rates if they rise

‚úÖ Solution with Ladder:

  • ‚úì Get portions back on schedule (yearly or every 6-18 months)
  • ‚úì If rates rise, reinvest maturing CDs at new higher rates
  • ‚úì Always have liquid access to some funds
  • ‚úì Better returns than single CD (due to compounding)
  • ‚úì No early withdrawal penalties (you wait for maturity)

‚ôªÔ∏è How Reinvestment Works

When a rung matures, you have choices:

Example: Year 1, First $10K Matures

Option A: Withdraw
Take the $10K (+ interest) as cash. Use it for emergencies or expenses.

Option B: Reinvest
Start a new 5-year CD with the matured funds (extends your ladder another 5 years).

Option C: Rate Advantage
If rates rose from 5.15% to 5.45%, reinvest at the better rate. Your ladder adapts!

Example Scenario: Rates Rise

Year 1: Locked in Navy Federal 1-yr CD @ 5.15%

Year 2: Rates have risen! Pentagon Federal now offers 5.45%

Your move: When the Year 1 CD matures, reinvest the funds at the new 5.45% rate

Result: Your ladder captures the rate improvement. You're not stuck at old rates.
